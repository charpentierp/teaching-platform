## Computing limits systematically

Rather than evaluating limits from scratch each time, we use a set of **limit laws** — algebraic rules that let us break complex limits into simpler pieces.

All the laws below assume that $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$ and $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = M$ both exist.

---

## The core limit laws

| Law | Statement |
|-----|-----------|
| **Sum** | $\lim [f(x) + g(x)] = L + M$ |
| **Difference** | $\lim [f(x) - g(x)] = L - M$ |
| **Constant multiple** | $\lim [c \cdot f(x)] = c \cdot L$ |
| **Product** | $\lim [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = L \cdot M$ |
| **Quotient** | $\lim \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \dfrac{L}{M}$, provided $M \neq 0$ |
| **Power** | $\lim [f(x)]^n = L^n$ |
| **Root** | $\lim \sqrt[n]{f(x)} = \sqrt[n]{L}$, provided $L > 0$ for even $n$ |

---

## Direct substitution

For **polynomial and rational functions**, if the function is defined at $a$, you can simply substitute:

$$\lim_{x \to a} p(x) = p(a)$$

**Example:**

$$\lim_{x \to 3} (2x^2 - 5x + 1) = 2(9) - 15 + 1 = 4$$

---

## Indeterminate forms — the $\frac{0}{0}$ case

When direct substitution gives $\frac{0}{0}$, the quotient law doesn't apply. Common strategies:

### 1. Factoring

$$\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2} = \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-2} = \lim_{x \to 2} (x+2) = 4$$

### 2. Rationalising (conjugate multiplication)

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{x+4} - 2}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{(\sqrt{x+4} - 2)(\sqrt{x+4} + 2)}{x(\sqrt{x+4} + 2)} = \frac{1}{4}$$

---

## The Squeeze Theorem

If $g(x) \leq f(x) \leq h(x)$ near $a$, and:

$$\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = \lim_{x \to a} h(x) = L$$

then $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$.

**Classic application:**

$$\lim_{x \to 0} x^2 \sin\!\left(\frac{1}{x}\right) = 0$$

Because $-x^2 \leq x^2 \sin(1/x) \leq x^2$, and both bounds go to 0.

---

## Two essential trigonometric limits

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \qquad \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0$$

Both follow from the Squeeze Theorem.
